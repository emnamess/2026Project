# Tunisian Artisanal E-Commerce - Design Direction

Date: 2026-06-11

## Design Goal

Create a minimalist storefront inspired by the clean, performance-focused feel of Gymshark, but with a subtle Tunisian identity. The visual tone should stay modern, spacious, and highly commercial, while only lightly referencing Tunisian tradition so the products remain the focus.

## Core Design Principles

- Minimalist first: reduce visual noise and keep layouts clean.
- Product-led: imagery, pricing, and trust signals should dominate.
- Premium but approachable: polished enough to feel modern, not luxury-heavy.
- Tunisian touch, not decoration overload: use tradition as an accent, not the main theme.
- Fast and responsive: design for mobile first and keep interactions lightweight.

## Visual Style

### What to borrow from the Gymshark style

- Large clean whitespace.
- Strong typography hierarchy.
- Simple grid-based product layouts.
- Dark/neutral base with restrained contrast.
- Minimal decorative elements.
- Focus on sharp product photography.

### Tunisian identity accents

Use only a small amount of local character through:
- Subtle geometric motifs inspired by Tunisian craft or tile patterns.
- Warm accent colors derived from earth, clay, sand, olive, or saffron tones.
- Small ornamental divider lines or corner details.
- Occasional background textures with very low contrast.
- Seasonal or editorial sections that reference heritage and handmade value.

Avoid:
- Heavy patterns across the whole site.
- Overly traditional framing that makes the site feel old-fashioned.
- Too many colors or visual effects.
- Decorative elements that compete with product images.

## Color Direction

Recommended palette approach:
- Base: off-white, charcoal, warm gray.
- Accent: one muted Tunisian-inspired color such as terracotta, olive, or brass.
- Support: one secondary neutral for sections and borders.

The palette should stay limited. One strong accent is enough.

## Typography

- Use a modern sans-serif for the main interface.
- Prefer strong headings, medium body text, and tight spacing.
- Arabic typography should remain highly legible and balanced with the Latin typeface.
- French should be the default language in the UI hierarchy.

## Layout System

- Wide desktop layout with generous spacing.
- Mobile-first responsive behavior.
- Product cards should be simple and consistent.
- Navigation should be short and direct.
- Homepage should feel curated, not crowded.

## Homepage Direction

Suggested structure:
- Minimal hero with one strong product/lifestyle image.
- Short value proposition in French.
- Featured categories.
- Best sellers or new arrivals.
- One subtle Tunisian craft/story section.
- Trust strip with shipping, payment, and support info.

## Product Page Direction

- Gallery first.
- Clear price and CTA.
- Straightforward product information.
- Minimal distractions.
- Recommendations below the fold.
- Lightweight accordion sections for details, shipping, and care.

## Tunisian Tradition Usage

Use tradition in small doses:
- On section separators.
- In the background of story/editorial blocks.
- In one accent color or motif system.
- In packaging-style visual cues.
- In photography and copy more than in decoration.

The main impression should still be modern e-commerce.

## Motion

- Keep motion subtle.
- Use small fades, gentle hover states, and light page transitions.
- Avoid flashy animations.
- Motion should help clarity, not become part of the identity.

## Photography Direction

- Use clean, high-resolution product photography.
- Favor natural light or controlled studio lighting.
- Keep backgrounds neutral.
- Show material texture clearly, since the products are traditional and tactile.

## Accessibility

- Strong contrast for all text.
- Clear focus states.
- Readable Arabic and French at all breakpoints.
- Touch-friendly spacing on mobile.
- No important information communicated only by color.

## Technical Design Notes

- Use a shared design token system for spacing, color, typography, and radius.
- Keep components reusable and simple.
- Avoid heavy client-side animation libraries unless needed.
- Optimize images aggressively for speed.

## Overall Direction Summary

The ideal direction is: a minimalist, modern storefront with excellent product presentation, subtle Tunisian cues, and no visual clutter. The site should feel like a premium direct-to-consumer brand, not a decorative cultural showcase.
